Spices for life

A spice is a seed, root, bark, berry, bud or other vegetable substance - which distinguished from herbs

The spice trade developed throughout in South Asia and Middle East by at least 2000 BCE

The flavor of a spice is derived in part from compounds (volatile oils) that oxidize or evaporate when exposed to air. Grinding a spice greatly increases its surface area and so increases the rates of oxidation and evaporation

Flavor is maximized by storing a spice whole and grinding when needed. The shelf life of a whole dry spice is roughly two years The "flavor life" of a ground spice can be much shorter
